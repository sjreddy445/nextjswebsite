    What Are The Top 5 Soft Skills Recruiters Look For

    The behavioural skills that are essential to survive & succeed in personal, professional & social life are usually known as soft skills. If hard skills are the foundation, soft skills are the cement. In fact, a judicious blend of both hard and soft skills is essential to achieve your professional success and fast-track your career.

    So, what are the top 5 soft skills recruiters look for in candidates today?

    1. Resilience & Agility
    Fluctuating market environments and the pandemic have taught us that candidates who are willing to adapt quickly to the changing needs of the business are priceless.

    2. Problem-Solving Skills
    Glitches, bumps in the road, and stumbling blocks are all part of the job. It’s essential to take them in stride and accept them as learning opportunities. One’s ability to use their knowledge to find answers to pressing problems and formulate workable solutions will demonstrate that you can handle – and excel in – your job.

    3. Leadership
    In times like these where work-from-home is the norm, every organization wants employees to be leaders in themselves. They look for the ones who can grow themselves and lend a helping hand to others. They want employees who can cultivate relationships up, down, and across the organizational chain.

    4. Communication
    As someone rightly said, successful communication involves five components—verbal, non-verbal, written, visual, and active listening. And the last one is the most essential in these times where we are mostly communicating over video and voice calls. You need to be able to listen to understand how to best communicate with someone.

    5. Work Ethic
    Today, organizations' are looking for employees who respect their jobs and are ready to take accountability for all they do. In hybrid/ WFH environments, there’s no one managing or micromanaging your day, but it is crucial to draw the line between our professional and personal lives. Flexible workspaces and timings are the norms today, but it is important to understand the seriousness of your work and meet deadlines effectively.

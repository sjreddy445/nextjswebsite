    HR tech trends: Why 2016 could be a game changer

    Digitization has swept the world; IoT seems more real than it ever did. HR is no exception to the influence that digital technologies are having on various aspects of business and life. A new set of entrants driven by fresh-eyed engineers are challenging the status quo and traditional HR systems are being replaced with new-age technologies. HR is being SMACed.

    Few trends that are going to have a lasting impact on HR as a function:

    • Robotic Automation and artificial intelligence in hr will be deployed to undertake processes in the bottom end of the pyramid thereby freeing up HR resources to focus on more cognitive, intelligent activities.
    • Mobile First is the order of the day with HR systems being applied. Any time, anywhere will drive employee feedback, leave, expense, goal setting, et al.
    • Technology in HR has been around for a long time but the challenge that most companies face is in its adoption. UI and UX will be major determining factors in HR technology investments. Consumerized experience – Employees expect the same quality of experience with systems as they have outside of work.
    • Cost efficiency and improved security is leading to many HR systems being moved to the Cloud. Companies have begun shifting their performance management and recruitment processes to cloud-based software.
    • People Analytics will elevate HR to the position of strategic business partner. From traditional dashboards, the trend is moving to advanced predictive analytics with recommendations. Analytics algorithms that predict flight risk, identify high potential employees, detect toxic employee behavior and more are gaining popularity.
    • HR systems are being gamified to build engagement and improve usage of HR systems. Check-ins, leader boards, point systems, etc. are some of the ways HR is enlivening their practices.
    • Companies are using technology to help their employees be more productive at work, despite growing demands on their time. Employee Social Networks are one of those tools that help fast and easy access to information, better problem solving, improved innovation, flexible learning, among others.

    While 2015 saw CHROs talking about some of these trends, 2016 will be the year of wider adoption. It will be imperative for HR professionals to upskill to ride this digitization wave else they will trail and lose out on an opportunity to become game-changers.

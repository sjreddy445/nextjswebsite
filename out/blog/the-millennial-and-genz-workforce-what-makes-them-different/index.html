    The Millennial & Genz Workforce: What Makes Them Different?

    Millennials & GenZ’s are probably the most maligned group in the professional circle. By 2025, the generation of Millennials will comprise 75% of the global workforce. And together with Generation Z, they will dominate the workplace in the not too distant future.

    For HR, this means that it’s essential to know some of the characteristics of these generations – think of their work styles, values, and beliefs – of workers to provide them with an optimal work environment.

    So, what are the top 5 ways to ensure their engagement within the organization?

    • Talk more about passion and corporate branding than about the money
      - 92% of Millennials believe that business should be about more than just profits
      - 30% of Born Digital employees would leave a role if they felt the organization lacked purpose
    • Collaborative Work Environments
      Both Millennials & GenZ’s believe teamwork is an essential aspect of building a high-performance organization and look to encourage and promote this working style.
    • Flexible Working
      For them, the new working world is all about choice. 82% of born-digital want employers who give them autonomy. Although a five-day week is still popular, they believe that they should be allowed to work a four-day week if they choose. They also want to decide when, where, how, and how long they would like to work.
    • Leverage Technology
      In a world of distributed work, tech tools and messaging platforms are critical for maintaining a sense of company culture and enabling effective teamwork.
      Only 26% of business leaders are optimistic about using instant messaging apps for work, compared to 82% of Born Digital workers and 83% of Millennial employees.
    • Effective Dialogue & Feedback
      This group doesn’t believe in annual feedback or dialogue during appraisals. They believe that they, the organization and their peers will only grow if there is an effective communication channel the whole year-round. Building employee confidence is a great way to help get and keep them motivated and enthusiastic. Also, what gets recognized gets repeated, which means creating a culture of recognition and performance goes a long way in motivating them.


    The Millennials and GenZ prioritize empathy, human values and employee well-being more than profitability. Thus, HR needs to thread their path effectively to hire and retain them.

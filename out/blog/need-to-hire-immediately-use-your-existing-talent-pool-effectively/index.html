    Need To Hire Immediately? Use Your Existing Talent Pool Effectively.

    Need to fill a position within the shortest time possible? Your reporting and hiring managers are following up relentlessly to close the position at the earliest? How do you deal with such a situation?

    You do not have any more time to hire from external sources, and the resumes received in the past few weeks haven't met your expectations. What do you do?

    Well, you may not tend to realize but the resume pool that you've created over the years is one of your most valuable assets, but you're not making the most out of it. It is a potential goldmine that, when used optimally, will save significant time and effort. According to an industry report, 67% of large companies and 56% of medium-sized organizations have difficulty filling positions.

    This repository of different resumes collected over time has been created either through career sites, employer profiles, or recruiters themselves going and getting some resumes. So, organizations have a repository, but you can think of it as more or less just a database, which was never maintained. You would find a resume lying in there, which was acquired some time ago but never used. Either the applicant didn't meet the job criteria, was a second choice to the position, or were not available. It means that the candidate was not hired for the particular position, and their resume is just lying in the repository without any meaningful use.

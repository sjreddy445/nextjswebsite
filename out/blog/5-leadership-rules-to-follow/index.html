    5 Leadership Rules To Follow

    "A leader is one who knows the way, goes the way & shows the way." — John Maxwell.

    Many think that leadership is a tough nut to crack, and rightly so. Whatever a leader does is scrutinised. If you do it too much, you're a control freak; if you do it less, you're not serious.

    Where do you draw the line? What can you do to be known and respected as a leader, not a boss?

    Here are our top 5 rules to follow.

    1. Give credit where it's due - Be it your employees or peers, give credit to them for the hard work they've put in. It will increase their trust and respect for you. Don't brag only about yourself.

    2. Don't follow the crowd - Don't go where others are walking. Pave your path. If you know, you're right - walk the path even if you're alone.

    3. Take risks - You can only succeed if you move out of your comfort zone. Only then will you be able to achieve success.

    4. Own your failures - You will fail at some point, and there are no two thoughts about it. However, it is essential to own them. Don't play a blame game. Sit back, introspect where you went wrong and try not to repeat them.

    5. Understand 'metrics culture' - Everyone loves company culture. However, metrics is something not everyone has clarity about. Culture has usually been a subjective topic with no measurability or tracking. It's essential to promote this culture in the organization so that each one is aware of organizational goals and how to achieve them.

    How HR Assumed Leadership Role In The Pandemic?

    While all of us may dream of pre-Covid office spaces, the fact is that the pandemic has drastically altered workplaces, and it will never go back to the same again. While organizations are making an effort to provide in-office capabilities, it will only be restricted to 2-3 times a week. The impacts on business, people, economy, mental health, and well-being have been significant. We have seen HR step up to these challenges and support their workforces and organizations through this crisis. It has taken a pandemic for many CEOs and employers to know the value their HR leader can bring as they have navigated these challenges.

    According to Gartner, there were 5 key priorities that HR needed to solve

    • Building critical skills and competencies - 68%
    • Current and future leadership bench - 44%
    • Employee Experience - 28%
    • Future of work - 32%
    • Organisational design and change management - 46%


      There are 6 ways in which they ensured the same.

    • Understanding the critical drivers of the business
    • Taking timely steps to mitigate challenges of the business
    • Supporting employees with empathy
    • Maintaining integrity whilst addressing business concerns
    • Role modelling the behaviours expected out of their workforce
    • Enriching employee experience

    To put it in a nutshell, their roles were moved from executive to leadership and will remain thus. Hence they must take the learnings from this pandemic and demonstrate the clear benefits of an engaged and productive workforce. As the immediate pressures of the pandemic start to ease, it will be necessary for HR leaders to capitalize on their newfound respect and visibility. CEOs and organizations need HR and need it now, more than ever.
